# Guest Happiness Guide (JWE2)

## Guest Types
There are four guest types. Each is attracted by different conditions and prefers different amenity products. Mixing guest types allows broader amenity configuration and higher profits.

| Type | Attracted By | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| General | High dinosaur count | Easiest to attract early game; low amenity value |
| Nature | Dinosaur comfort and variety | Good mid-game focus; naturally accumulates |
| Luxury | Hotels, accommodation rating | Requires investment in hotel infrastructure |
| Adventure | High infamy dinosaurs, carnivore activity | Hardest to satisfy; requires dangerous park conditions |

## Guest Comfort
Comfort is tracked separately from guest approval. It is found in the Manager View → Park Rating → Guests. It is determined by four factors:

| Factor | How to Improve |
|---|---|
| Restroom coverage | Place restrooms where paths show red |
| Shelter coverage | Place emergency shelters throughout the park |
| Accommodation rating | Build and upgrade hotels with dinosaur views |
| Transport rating | Build monorails and tour routes |

Hotels boost comfort substantially when they have line-of-sight to dinosaur enclosures. The more species visible, the better.

## Amenities
Amenities are the primary profit source and the only place guests give direct feedback. There are three types: Food, Drink, and Shopping. Each can be configured with different products targeting different guest types.

**Placement:** Place amenities where paths show red when in placement mode — this indicates high demand. Amenities placed far from attractions will not generate profit regardless of demand indicators.

**Configuration:** Each product has a cost and targets specific guest types. Match products to your dominant guest type for maximum profit. Multi-type products cost more but serve everyone.

**Staffing:** If an amenity is at guest capacity, add more staff rather than building more amenities. A fully-staffed amenity outperforms two understaffed ones.

**Pricing:** Around 3× the base price is a reliable profitable rate without alienating guests.

**Timing:** Don't configure amenity modules until the park has enough guests to sustain them — operating costs will exceed revenue with low footfall.

## Park Rating
Park rating is primarily driven by income. The two main contributors are ticket sales and amenity revenue. Higher park rating unlocks more content in Challenge mode.

- Maximise dinosaur appeal (variety + visibility) to boost ticket sales
- Ensure full amenity coverage near all attractions
- Specialising for a dominant guest type lets you configure amenities for higher per-unit profit

## Guest Safety
Guest deaths from dinosaur escapes cause rapid financial loss. Storms can quickly send parks bankrupt if enclosures are compromised. Prioritise fence strength and emergency shelters as park size grows.

## Quick Reference: What Guests Want
- **All guests:** visible dinosaurs, restrooms, shelters, transport
- **Nature guests:** high dinosaur comfort, species variety
- **Luxury guests:** hotels near enclosures, high accommodation rating
- **Adventure guests:** high infamy dinosaurs, carnivore kills and fence breaks (see Infamy guide)
