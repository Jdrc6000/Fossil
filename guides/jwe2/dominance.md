# Dominance Guide (JWE2)

## How Dominance Works
Every dinosaur has a dominance rating calculated from its attack and defense stats, plus any traits it carries. The dinosaur with the highest dominance in a pack is expected to be the Alpha — indicated by a crown icon on its status window. If it isn't the Alpha, it will repeatedly challenge whoever is until it wins or the loop is broken.

Dominance fights are rarely lethal but always cause injuries requiring Paleo-Medical treatment. A good Alpha establishes order in one fight or less and is never challenged again.

**As of update 1.4.0**, dominance fights are decided by dominance rating rather than raw combat stats, reducing repeat fighting.

## Traits That Affect Dominance

| Trait | Dominance Effect | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Aggressive | +35% dominance | Also increases vehicle attack chance |
| Social | +30% dominance | Pack-wide benefit if Alpha has it |
| Antisocial | +30% dominance | Same bonus as Social; loner behaviour |
| Strong | +25 attack → dominance increase | Boosts combat ability too |
| Defensive | +25 defense → dominance increase | Boosts combat survivability |
| Humble | High dominance | Good alpha trait; pack-wide calming effect |
| Tolerant | No dominance boost | +30% cohabitation comfort with neutral species |

## The Alpha Rules
1. All pack members at equal dominance → no fights; the first released becomes Alpha
2. Alpha has higher dominance than all others → stable, no challenges
3. Alpha has lower dominance than a pack member → that member fights repeatedly until it wins or the Alpha's combat stats let it keep winning (causing an infinite fight loop)
4. A non-dominant dino wins a fight → it becomes the new Alpha

## Building a Stable Pack

**The Alpha** should be incubated with traits that boost dominance AND combat (e.g. Humble + Strong, or Social + Strong). Social and Humble on the Alpha apply pack-wide effects, stabilising the whole enclosure. Release the Alpha first — the first dinosaur released becomes Alpha by default.

**Pack members** should have no dominance-boosting traits at all — avoid Aggressive, Social, Antisocial, Strong, and Defensive on non-Alpha animals. Traits are RNG on synthesis; modify the genome to increase the chance of desired traits and batch-synthesise until you get what you want.

**Equal dominance** is a valid alternative strategy: release all dinosaurs with identical dominance values (same traits or no traits) and the first released becomes a stable Alpha with no conflict.

## Infamy and Dominance
Infamy levels add +20% dominance per level. A high-infamy Alpha is significantly harder to dethrone. See the Infamy guide for details.

## Common Problems

| Problem | Cause | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Infinite fight loop | Non-Alpha has higher dominance but equal attack | Give Alpha higher attack, or equalise all dominance |
| New dino destabilises pack | It has higher dominance than current Alpha | Don't release it; sell or move to quarantine |
| Fights after adding a new animal | New animal has a dominance-boosting trait | Batch-synthesise pack members without those traits |
| Alpha keeps losing challenges | Challenger has same attack stat | Boost Alpha attack via genome modification |
