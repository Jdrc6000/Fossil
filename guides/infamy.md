# Infamy Guide (JWE2)

## What Infamy Does
- Attracts Adventure guests
- Boosts Dominance (+20% per level)
- Has 5 levels: Harmless (Lv0), through to Savage (Lv4)

## How Infamy Works: The Checklist
Neutral (baseline) infamy is determined by a checklist of events a dinosaur has experienced. Each item permanently raises the baseline level:

| Event | Effect |
|---|---|
| Won a fight | +1 step |
| Killed a dinosaur in combat | +1 step |
| Hunted a dinosaur (predator kill) | +1 step |
| Hunted a human (guest kill) | +1 step |
| Broken a fence | +1 step |

- Scars and lost fights do **not** affect infamy
- Infamy decays or recovers toward this baseline over time
- Baseline varies per individual within a species, independent of traits

## Infamy Levels
- Each level above baseline is reached at 50%, then 100%
- Breaking a fence shifts current infamy to 50% of current level (e.g. 100% Lv3 → 50% Lv3)
- Dominance values may not display correctly until save/reload — this is a visual bug

## Practical Uses

### Filling the Checklist
Introduce other dinosaurs into the pen, or arrange a deliberate "transport incident" to give your dinosaur a kill. This satisfies Adventure guests and stabilises enclosure hierarchy.

### Managing Your Alpha
Take your Alpha (with a low-dominance hunting buddy) on quarantine pen hunting trips. Killing a few Dracorex or Struthiomimus fills the checklist and reinforces dominance over subordinates.

### Herbivore Infamy
Herbivores benefit too — an ankylosaur or trike that has killed a carnivore will be significantly more dominant and stable.

### Problem Dinosaurs
If a dinosaur is disruptive despite low-dominance traits, check their infamy checklist. Man-eaters and fence-breakers accumulate infamy fast, making them harder to control. Options:
- Match their infamy by boosting your Alpha's checklist equally
- Remove the problem dinosaur entirely
