# Dinosaur Traits Guide (JWE2)

## Overview
Traits are inherited randomly during egg synthesis. They are not guaranteed — you modify the genome to adjust the *probability* of a trait appearing, then batch-synthesise until you get the result you want. A dinosaur can have up to 3 traits. Genome completion percentage determines how many gene modification slots are available; 100% genome unlocks the maximum slots.

Higher genome modification increases the chance of the Aggressive trait appearing as a side effect. Keeping Social modifications at ~75% rather than 100% reduces unwanted Aggressive trait rolls.

Negative traits can appear regardless of genome modifications, especially at lower viability percentages.

## Behavioural Traits

| Trait | Effect | Use |
|---|---|---|
| Aggressive | +35% dominance; more likely to attack vehicles | Alpha only — never use on pack members |
| Social | +30% dominance; pack-wide comfort boost if Alpha has it | Excellent Alpha trait |
| Antisocial | +30% dominance; +30% social need, -30% socialise chance | Valid Alpha alternative; loner behaviour |
| Humble | High dominance; pack-wide calming effect | Best general Alpha trait |
| Tolerant | +30% cohabitation comfort with neutral species | Pack members in mixed enclosures |
| Intolerant | -30% cohabitation threshold | Negative trait; causes cohabitation issues |
| Nocturnal | More active at night | Mostly cosmetic; minor behaviour shifts |
| Needy | Increased hunger/thirst needs | Negative trait; more frequent feeding required |
| Long Lived | Extended lifespan | Useful for high-value or hard-to-replace dinosaurs |

## Combat Traits

| Trait | Effect |
|---|---|
| Strong | +25 attack; dominance increase |
| Defensive | +25 defense; dominance increase |
| Weak | Reduced attack; higher injury risk in fights |
| Fit | Increased fitness/stamina |

## Genome Modifications (Gene Splicing)
Gene modifications are applied in the Hatchery before synthesis. They affect base stats (attack, defense, lifespan, resilience) and adjust the probability of traits appearing.

### Modification Tiers
- **Single trait mods** (e.g. Skin Toughness, Tooth Hardness): boost one stat; low viability cost; safe to stack
- **Double trait mods** (e.g. Intensive Repair, Brain Ageing): boost two stats; moderate viability cost; reliable mid-game option
- **Triple trait mods** (e.g. Increased Bone Density, Strong Muscle Fibers): boost three stats; highest rating increase; highest viability cost; not upgradeable

### Social/Comfort Modifications (Dr. Wu DLC)
These modify enclosure and social thresholds rather than combat stats:
- **Confined Space Adaptation**: reduces grassland/forest requirements (smaller enclosures viable)
- **Solitary Adaptation**: lowers social minimums and maximums
- **Group Adaptation**: raises social requirements (needs larger groups)
- **Cross Species Adaptation**: raises population tolerances (better cohabitation)
- **Single Species Adaptation**: lowers population tolerance (prefers solo species enclosures)
- **Rapid Stress Response**: raises the comfort threshold at which a dinosaur becomes dangerous

## Viability and Incubation
Every gene modification reduces viability (hatch success rate). Hatchery upgrades can increase success rate. At low viability, incubations are more likely to fail and negative traits are more likely to appear. Use the minimum modifications needed for your goal.

## Alpha Genome Strategy
1. Modify genome for Strong or Defensive (combat edge) + Social or Humble (dominance + pack effect)
2. Synthesise a batch; select the egg with Strong + Humble (or Social + Strong)
3. Release this dinosaur first
4. Reset genome to no dominance-boosting traits before synthesising pack members
5. Batch synthesise pack members; avoid any egg with Aggressive, Social, Antisocial, Strong, or Defensive traits

## Biome Trait Effects (Update 1.5.0)
Certain biomes increase the chance of specific negative traits:
- Alpine and Taiga biomes: increased chance of Hungry trait
- Desert biomes: increased chance of Thirsty trait
