# Dominance Guide (JWE3)

## How Dominance Works
Every dinosaur has a dominance rating calculated from its combat stats (attack and defence), its Infamy level, and any traits it carries. The dinosaur with the highest dominance in a Territory will attempt to become the Alpha by challenging the current one. Dominance fights cause injuries requiring MVU treatment and, in severe cases, can be fatal.

A stable Alpha establishes order quickly and is rarely or never challenged again. An unstable hierarchy results in repeated fights, mounting medical bills, and stressed animals.

## Traits That Affect Dominance

| Trait | Dominance Effect | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Aggressive | +35% dominance | Also increases vehicle attack chance |
| Social | +30% dominance | Pack-wide comfort bonus if Alpha has it |
| Antisocial | +30% dominance | Same bonus as Social; loner behaviour |
| Strong | +25 attack → dominance increase | Boosts combat effectiveness too |
| Defensive | +25 defence → dominance increase | Boosts combat survivability too |
| Humble | High dominance | Good Alpha trait; pack-wide calming effect |
| Skittish | Reduces fight-initiation chance | Excellent pack-member trait |
| Tolerant | No dominance effect | +30% cohabitation comfort with neutral species |

## The Alpha Rules
1. All pack members at equal dominance → no fights; the first dinosaur released becomes Alpha by default
2. Alpha has higher dominance than all others → stable, challenges unlikely
3. Alpha has lower dominance than a pack member → that member fights repeatedly until it wins or is outcompeted in combat stats
4. A challenger with the Social trait may still initiate fights even against a clearly dominant Alpha — from testing, the chance of a fight is never truly zero when a dominance-boosting trait is present on a pack member, even at low dominance values

**Release the Alpha first.** The first dinosaur released into an enclosure becomes the default Alpha. If pack members are released before the intended Alpha, one of them may establish dominance first, triggering fights when the true Alpha is released.

**After tranquilising and moving dinosaurs**, they may re-establish dominance as if new to the enclosure. Expect a brief period of re-settling after any transport.

## Building a Stable Pack

**The Alpha** should be synthesised with traits that boost both dominance and combat stats — Humble + Strong or Social + Strong are reliable combinations. Social and Humble on the Alpha apply effects to the whole enclosure. Release the Alpha before any other pack members.

**Pack members** should carry no dominance-boosting or combat-boosting traits at all. Avoid Aggressive, Social, Antisocial, Strong, and Defensive on non-Alpha animals. The Skittish trait is ideal for pack members as it reduces the chance of them initiating challenges. Traits are random on synthesis, so modify the genome to reduce the likelihood of unwanted traits appearing and batch-synthesise until suitable eggs are available.

**Equal dominance** is a viable alternative: release all dinosaurs with identical dominance values and the first released becomes a stable Alpha with no conflict. This is the simplest approach for species where trait-hunting is impractical.

## Infamy and Dominance
Infamy contributes to dominance. A highly infamous Alpha becomes significantly harder to dethrone over time. See the Infamy guide for details on how to build and manage Infamy deliberately.

## Juveniles and Dominance
Parents will aggressively defend juveniles against any dinosaur perceived as a threat. This includes pack members of the same species. Factor parental aggression into enclosure planning when juveniles are present. Some species are more hostile toward their own young than others — check the species' parenting style before breeding.

## Cohabitation Conflicts
Dominance fights can occur not just within species but between species sharing an enclosure. Disliked species will fight to the death and should never share space. Neutral species raise cohabitation stress and may trigger fights if too many are present together. Check the Territory tab of any dinosaur to see its Liked, Neutral, and Disliked species before adding a cohab.

## Common Problems

| Problem | Cause | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Repeated challenges despite large dominance gap | Pack member has a dominance-boosting trait (e.g. Social) | Re-synthesise pack members without those traits |
| Fights restart after transport | Territory re-establishment period post-tranquilise | Allow settling time; ensure Alpha is released first in the new enclosure |
| New juvenile destabilises pack | Parents become aggressive in defence | Ensure all enclosure mates are Liked or Neutral species |
| Infinite fight loop | Challenger has equal or higher combat stats to the Alpha | Boost Alpha attack via Genetic Modification, or equalise all dominance |
| New dino triggers fighting after addition | New dino has higher dominance than current Alpha | Do not release it; sell or move to quarantine |
