# Guest Happiness Guide (JWE3)

## Overview
Guest experience in JWE3 is tracked through three distinct metrics: Park Appeal, Guest Comfort, and Guest Approval. All three contribute to your Potential Guest count — the number of guests your park can attract. Revenue scales directly with guest volume, so maximising all three is the path to a profitable park.

Open the Site Economy menu at any time to see a breakdown of each metric and identify the weakest areas.

## Park Appeal
Appeal is the primary draw that brings guests to your park in the first place. A higher Appeal score means more potential visitors. Appeal is calculated from three contributors:

**Visible Dinosaurs**: each species has a unique base Appeal value. Dinosaurs can only contribute Appeal if they are visible to guests from a Viewing Attraction (Viewing Gallery, Platform, Monorail, Hotel, Tour Ride, etc.). Use the Dinosaur Visibility View Mode to identify enclosures with poor viewing coverage. Appeal per dinosaur is boosted if it is a Juvenile, if it has been naturally bred, or if it was well cared for during its juvenile stage.

**Species Variety**: each park has a minimum Species Target. Falling short applies a penalty to Appeal; meeting and exceeding the target applies a bonus. Always prioritise variety alongside population count.

**Entertainment Attractions**: static Appeal sources (e.g. the Innovation Centre) and dynamic attractions that draw Appeal from nearby dinosaurs (Cretaceous Cruise, Balloon Tour, Dinosaur Encounter, Zipline). Dynamic attractions scale with the Appeal of the dinosaurs they can see, making their placement relative to high-Appeal enclosures critical.

### Boosting Appeal
- Build Viewing Galleries and Platforms with sight lines into enclosures
- Keep a variety of species; meet the Species Target for each park
- Breed dinosaurs naturally — naturally-born dinosaurs have higher base Appeal than Synthesised ones
- Care for juveniles well; adults raised with high childhood comfort carry a permanent Appeal bonus
- Place dynamic attractions adjacent to your highest-Appeal enclosures
- Use the Dinosaur Visibility View Mode regularly to find uncovered enclosure areas

## Guest Comfort
Comfort measures how well the physical needs of guests are met once they are in the park. It has four components:

| Factor | How to Improve |
|---|---|
| Restroom coverage | Place restrooms where the heat map shows red demand |
| Shelter coverage | Place emergency shelters throughout the park, especially near enclosures |
| Accommodation rating | Build and upgrade Hotels with sight lines to dinosaur enclosures |
| Transport rating | Build Monorails, tour routes, and Ziplines; connect areas of the park |

Hotels provide a strong comfort bonus when they have line-of-sight to enclosures. The more species visible from a hotel, the higher the accommodation rating contribution.

Ziplines serve double duty: they function as Viewing Attractions (boosting Appeal) and as Transport links (boosting Transport Rating).

Low Guest Comfort directly reduces Potential Guest count, counteracting any Appeal gains. Treat Comfort infrastructure as mandatory, not optional.

## Guest Approval
Approval reflects how safe and trustworthy guests perceive the park to be. It has four components:

| Factor | How it is Damaged | How to Recover |
|---|---|---|
| Dinosaur Welfare | Unhealthy, uncared-for animals | Scan and treat dinosaurs regularly; keep comfort high |
| Incident Prevention | Dinosaur escapes, guest injuries | Contain escapes quickly; use Capture Teams and Shelters |
| Safety Rating | Undeployed shelters; unresolved incidents | Ensure shelter coverage; resolve incidents without delay |
| Publicity Rating | Rapid bad decisions; repeated incidents | Allow time to pass; avoid chain incidents |

Incident Prevention and Publicity Rating recover passively over time if no new incidents occur. A single escape handled quickly does relatively little lasting damage; repeated escapes in a short window are much more damaging.

## Guest Demographics
JWE3 recognises four guest demographics. Attractions appeal to different demographics, and optimising your park layout around the dominant type in a given area increases revenue.

| Type | Primarily Attracted By | Best Viewing Attractions |
|---|---|---|
| General | High dinosaur count | Viewing Platforms, Galleries |
| Nature | Dinosaur comfort and species variety | Viewing Platforms, Galleries, natural enclosures |
| Luxury | Hotels and high accommodation rating | Hotels, Monorail |
| Adventure | High-Infamy dinosaurs, carnivore activity | Cretaceous Cruise, Dinosaur Encounter, Balloon Tour near predator enclosures |

Different Viewing Attractions attract different demographic concentrations. Check the Attractions Guide for which attractions draw which types.

## Amenities
Amenities (Food, Drink, Shopping) are the primary revenue source beyond ticket sales. Placement and configuration both matter.

**Placement**: place amenities where path heat maps show red. Amenities far from congested areas generate little revenue regardless of quality.

**Pricing**: roughly 3× the base item price is a reliable profitable rate without alienating guests.

**Staffing**: a fully-staffed amenity outperforms two understaffed ones. Add staff rather than building duplicates when an amenity hits capacity.

**Timing**: do not configure amenity modules until guest volume is sufficient to cover operating costs. Running high-cost modules on low footfall is a net loss.

**Configuration**: match products to the dominant guest demographic in that area for maximum per-unit profit.

## Path Congestion
High-Appeal attractions draw crowds. Congested paths reduce guest satisfaction and can cap effective visitor throughput. Widen paths near major Viewing Attractions, provide multiple route options, and use Ziplines and Monorails to distribute guests across the park rather than funnelling them all through one corridor.

## Quick Reference

| Goal | Action |
|---|---|
| More guests | Increase Appeal: more visible species, better variety, natural breeding |
| Higher revenue per guest | Amenities near high-footfall attractions; correct pricing and staffing |
| Better comfort scores | Add restrooms, shelters, hotels with enclosure views, transport connections |
| Recover from an incident | Contain it quickly, deploy shelters, treat animals; wait for Publicity to recover |
| Attract Adventure guests | Build high-Infamy predator enclosures; place tours near them |
| Attract Luxury guests | Invest in Hotels with dinosaur views and high accommodation rating |
