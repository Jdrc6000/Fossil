# Infamy Guide (JWE3)

## What Infamy Does
- Contributes to a dinosaur's Dominance rating
- Attracts Adventure guests to your park
- Increases individual Appeal Rating
- Has 5 levels ranging from Harmless (Level 0) to Savage (Level 4)

## How Infamy Works
Infamy is tracked per individual dinosaur. Infamous acts raise a dinosaur's minimum Infamy baseline permanently — the baseline is the floor that Infamy decays back to when the animal is calm. Current Infamy decays toward the baseline over time.

### Infamous Acts (each raises the permanent baseline)
| Act | Effect |
|---|---|
| Won a dominance fight | +1 step to baseline |
| Killed another dinosaur in combat | +1 step to baseline |
| Hunted prey (predator kill) | +1 step to baseline |
| Killed a guest | +1 step to baseline |
| Broke out of an enclosure | +1 step to baseline |
| Gained a scar | Minor increase |

Losing fights does **not** raise Infamy.

Current Infamy above baseline decays passively over time. Once a baseline step is earned, it is permanent for that individual — it cannot be removed.

## Infamy Levels
- Each level has two thresholds: 50% (midpoint) and 100% (next level)
- Breaking a fence resets current Infamy to 50% of the current level — it is a destabilising act even if the animal is recaptured
- High Infamy contributes meaningfully to Dominance: higher-Infamy animals are harder to challenge and dethrone

**Visual bug note**: Dominance values may not display correctly in the UI until after a save and reload. If numbers look wrong, save and reload to force the display to update.

## Practical Applications

### Filling the Infamy Checklist for Adventure Guest Appeal
Arrange situations that allow your target dinosaur to earn Infamy safely:
- Introduce a live prey animal to a carnivore's enclosure and allow a hunt
- Set up a controlled dominance encounter where your target wins
- For large carnivore Alphas, periodic prey feeding is both a hunger management strategy and an Infamy builder

### Strengthening an Alpha's Dominance
An Alpha that has filled its Infamy checklist is significantly harder to dethrone than a freshly synthesised one of the same species and traits. If a dominant challenger is causing repeated fights despite your Alpha having appropriate traits, consider whether the challenger has accumulated more Infamy — and either match it on your Alpha through deliberate acts or remove the problem animal.

### Herbivore Infamy
Herbivores benefit from Infamy too. A Triceratops or Ankylosaur that has killed a carnivore attacker gains substantial Infamy, boosting its Dominance and Appeal. This is especially useful for stabilising a herd with a powerful, hard-to-challenge Alpha.

### Adventure Guest Optimisation
Adventure guests are drawn to enclosures with high-Infamy animals and to incidents involving carnivore activity and fence breaks. Place tour attractions (Cretaceous Cruise, Balloon Tour, Dinosaur Encounter) adjacent to predator enclosures with infamous residents. A Tyrannosaur at Savage Infamy is one of the highest natural sources of Adventure guest attraction in the game.

### Managing Problem Animals
If a dinosaur accumulates Infamy through repeated escapes or guest incidents (man-eaters), it becomes progressively more dominant and harder to manage. Options:
- Raise your Alpha's Infamy to match via deliberate acts, re-establishing dominance
- Transfer the problem animal to a quarantine pen or a dedicated high-security enclosure
- Sell or rewild the animal if it has become unmanageable

## Infamy and Juveniles
Juveniles can accumulate Infamy just like adults — they can attack guests and escape enclosures if given the opportunity. A juvenile with early Infamy will mature into a more dominant adult. This can be beneficial (breeding powerful future Alphas) or problematic (disrupting stable packs) depending on your goals.

## Summary Table

| Goal | Method |
|---|---|
| Boost Alpha's dominance | Arrange fights, prey hunts, or controlled escapes for the Alpha |
| Attract Adventure guests | Build tour attractions near high-Infamy predator enclosures |
| Stabilise a herd | Give the Alpha more Infamy than any likely challenger |
| Handle a high-Infamy problem animal | Match Alpha Infamy, or remove/quarantine the problem animal |
| Raise herbivore dominance | Allow herbivore to defend against or kill a carnivore attacker |
