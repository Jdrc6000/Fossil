# Breeding Guide (JWE3)

## Overview
Breeding is the flagship new mechanic in JWE3. For the first time in the series, male and female dinosaurs exist separately, can form compatible pairs, and produce juvenile offspring that grow into adults within your park. Breeding offers three major benefits: growing your population without spending on Synthesis, generating naturally-bred dinosaurs that carry a permanent Appeal bonus, and shaping future generations through selective trait inheritance.

Around 75 of the 85+ base species are breedable.

## Two Paths to Breeding

### Hatchery (Synthesis)
The Hatchery is the laboratory approach. Fossils collected via Expeditions are analysed to build Genome progress. Once sufficient DNA is available, Scientists can Synthesise eggs at the Hatchery. Before incubation begins you can apply Genetic Modifications to influence traits, choose the dinosaur's sex, and customise its appearance (skin, pattern, and colouring). Select viable eggs from the batch — any manifested traits are visible at this stage — and begin incubation.

The Hatchery gives maximum control. It is the only reliable way to guarantee male dinosaurs, which are rarer but essential for any natural breeding programme. Use it to create your founding generation with the traits and genetics you want to propagate.

### Natural Breeding (Nesting Areas)
Natural breeding occurs when a compatible male and female of the same species share an enclosure with appropriate Nests, sufficient Territory, and satisfactory comfort. If conditions are right, they will eventually claim a nest and lay a clutch of eggs. You receive no control over the offspring's traits, sex, or appearance — those are determined entirely by the parents' genetics and inheritance.

Natural breeding produces offspring with a higher base Appeal Rating than Synthesised dinosaurs of the same species. A well-maintained lineage across multiple generations compounds this bonus further.

**Remove nests** from any enclosure where you want to stop breeding — this immediately halts new clutches.

## Breeding Requirements
For natural breeding to occur, all of the following must be true:
- At least one male and one female of the same species are in the enclosure
- Their comfort needs are met (green bars, no red)
- The Territory is large enough to accommodate the existing population and potential offspring
- An appropriate Nest is placed within the female's Territory
- The female is Fertile (check the Fertility tab on the female's info panel)
- The pair have sufficient Compatibility

Compatibility and Fertility are shown in each dinosaur's info panel. Low Fertility can be addressed through Genetic Modification (Fertility trait) and research. Low Compatibility can be raised through Structured Breeding Programmes.

## Structured Breeding Programmes
From any placed Nest, select Create Plan and choose an eligible male and female pair. A Scientist must be assigned to the programme. A Structured Breeding Programme boosts the pair's compatibility and their chance of producing a clutch, making it the best option when natural rates are low or when you want to target specific parent combinations for trait propagation.

The programme completes after a set time; if successful, the nest is claimed and eggs are laid. Failed programmes can be restarted.

## Males
Males are rare by default. Sources for acquiring males:
- **Synthesis via Hatchery**: once Male Synthesis is Researched at the Science Centre, you can select male sex during incubation
- **Capture**: wild dinosaurs near some campaign sites may be captured
- **Marketplace**: once unlocked, males can be purchased

Maintaining one or two males per species enclosure is sufficient for most breeding programmes. Having too many males in a Territory can raise social stress in some species — check the Social Needs tab for male count requirements.

## Juveniles
When eggs hatch, juveniles emerge directly into the enclosure and immediately join the existing population.

Juveniles have their own needs that may differ from adults. Scan them with your MVU Team shortly after hatching to catch diseases early and verify their comfort requirements. Key things to manage with juveniles:

- **Space**: juveniles grow into adults and increase Territory size requirements. Plan enclosures with adult population size in mind, not just current headcount.
- **Food access**: juveniles can be displaced from feeders by dominant adults. Place additional feeders if juveniles are showing hunger stress.
- **Safety**: juveniles will be attacked if they encounter Disliked or agitated species. Only cohabit juveniles with Liked or reliably Neutral species.
- **Parent aggression**: parents will fight any enclosure resident perceived as a threat to their young. This includes Neutral cohab species that stray too close.
- **Upbringing bonus**: juveniles that reach adulthood with a high Comfort Rating throughout their childhood carry a permanent Appeal boost as adults. Neglected juveniles lose this bonus permanently.

## Trait Inheritance
Offspring inherit traits, skin patterns, and colouring from both parents. Traits that are not expressed in one generation may still be carried as recessive genes and re-emerge in grandchildren or beyond. This has two practical implications:

1. Breeding two clean-trait parents together does not guarantee clean-trait offspring. Monitor every new clutch for unexpected negative traits.
2. Positive traits you introduce through Synthesis propagate naturally through generations without further Hatchery intervention — this is the most efficient long-term strategy for spreading desirable traits through a population.

Use contraceptives (available from the individual dinosaur's menu) on any animal whose traits you do not want passed on.

## Recommended Breeding Strategy
1. Synthesise a founding male with desirable traits (Fertile, Positive Temperament, Confined Space Adaptation, plus any combat or appeal traits relevant to the species) via the Hatchery.
2. Synthesise one or more founding females with complementary traits.
3. Place appropriate Nests. Build a Structured Breeding Programme for the founding pair to guarantee the first clutch.
4. Care for juveniles attentively — high childhood comfort converts directly into adult Appeal.
5. As the lineage matures, use Structured Breeding Programmes to select the best-trait adults as the next generation's parents.
6. Apply contraceptives to animals carrying negative traits to prevent those genes from spreading further.

## Dimorphism
Males and females are visually distinct within each species. Male pterosaurs, for example, have larger and more colourful crests than females. These differences are cosmetic but contribute to the realism and visual variety of your park. Guests respond positively to visible family groups.

## Sandbox Note
In Sandbox mode, juvenile maturity can be toggled on or off in the park settings. With maturity off, juveniles remain juveniles indefinitely — useful for display parks or screenshots.
