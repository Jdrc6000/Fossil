# Dinosaur Traits Guide (JWE3)

## Overview
Traits are characteristics that define how a dinosaur behaves, how hardy it is, and how appealing it is to guests. A dinosaur can acquire traits in three ways: through Genetic Modification before Synthesis in a Hatchery, through random manifestation during Synthesis, or through inheritance from parents during natural breeding.

Each species' Genome has predetermined chances for positive and negative traits. Genetic Modifications shift those probabilities but cannot guarantee a specific outcome — some traits are rare and will only appear randomly. The number of available modification slots increases with higher Genome progress; 100% Genome unlocks the maximum slots. Genome progress is advanced by collecting fossils through Expeditions and analysing them at the Science Centre.

Negative traits can appear regardless of modifications, especially at lower viability percentages. Keep viability as high as possible by not stacking too many modifications at once.

## Behavioural Traits

| Trait | Effect | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Aggressive | +35% dominance; increased vehicle attack chance | Only desirable on a pack Alpha |
| Social | +30% dominance; pack-wide comfort boost if Alpha has it | Good Alpha trait; avoid on pack members |
| Antisocial | +30% dominance; increased social needs, reduced socialise chance | Valid lone-Alpha option; risky on pack members |
| Humble | High dominance; park-wide calming effect | Best general Alpha trait |
| Skittish | Reduces likelihood of initiating dominance fights | Excellent pack-member trait; lowers conflict risk |
| Tolerant | +30% cohabitation comfort with neutral species | Key for mixed-species enclosures |
| Intolerant | Reduced cohabitation threshold | Negative trait; avoid in mixed enclosures |
| Nocturnal | More active at night | Mostly cosmetic |
| Needy | Increased hunger and thirst needs | Negative trait |
| Long Lived | Extended lifespan | Useful for high-value specimens |
| Intelligent | High Appeal Rating; actively looks for escape routes | High risk — keeps guests and food away from enclosure edges |

**Note on Intelligent:** Dinosaurs with the Intelligent trait will attempt to escape their enclosure whenever they spot food, prey, or guests near the fence line, regardless of their Comfort Rating. Use high-security fencing and keep paths clear of enclosure edges.

| Weak | Reduced attack; higher injury risk in fights |
| Fit | Increased fitness and stamina |

## Fertility and Breeding Traits

These traits affect the breeding system introduced in JWE3. They can be researched and applied via Genetic Modification, or inherited from parents.

| Trait | Effect |
|---|---|
| Fertile | Increased breeding chance |
| Compatible | Improves compatibility ratings with breeding partners |
| Cross-Species Adaptation | Better cohabitation tolerance; reduced stress from neutral species |
| Group Adaptation | Higher sociability; less stress from crowded enclosures |
| Positive Temperament | More adaptable to imperfect environments; fewer strict comfort requirements |
| Confined Space Adaptation | Reduced territory size requirements |

Fertility and Compatibility are the most important traits to research early if you intend to run a natural breeding programme. Confined Space Adaptation is highly recommended for parks with limited enclosure space.

## Genetic Modifications

Modifications are applied in the Hatchery before Synthesis and alter base stats (attack, defence, lifespan, resilience) as well as trait probabilities. New modifications are unlocked by researching at the Science Centre.

### Modification Tiers
- **Single-stat mods**: boost one characteristic; low viability cost; safe to stack
- **Double-stat mods**: boost two characteristics; moderate viability cost
- **Triple-stat mods**: boost three characteristics; highest rating increase; highest viability cost; not upgradeable

### Key Genetic Modifications
| Modification | Stats Boosted |
|---|---|
| Tooth Hardness | Attack |
| Skin Toughness | Defence |
| Intensive Repair | Defence + Resilience |
| Immune Response | Lifespan + Resilience |
| Increased Bone Density | Attack + Defence + Resilience |
| Strong Muscle Fibres | Attack + Defence + Lifespan |
| Rapid Metabolism | Defence + Lifespan + Resilience |
| Reaction Speed | Attack + Lifespan |
| Cardio Strength | Lifespan |

### Social and Comfort Modifications
- **Confined Space Adaptation**: reduces territory size requirements
- **Solitary Adaptation**: lowers social minimums and maximums
- **Group Adaptation**: raises social requirements; suits herd species
- **Cross-Species Adaptation**: improves cohabitation comfort with other species
- **Single Species Adaptation**: reduces population tolerance; prefers solo-species enclosures
- **Positive Temperament**: increases general comfort adaptability

## Trait Inheritance

In JWE3, traits are passed down through natural breeding. Offspring inherit traits, skin patterns, and colouring from their parents and ancestors. Traits that aren't expressed in a generation can still be carried and re-emerge in later descendants. This means a negative trait eliminated from the parent generation may reappear in grandchildren. Monitor new juveniles for unexpected traits, particularly Aggressive, Intolerant, or Intelligent, and use contraceptives on individuals whose traits you don't want passed on.

## Viability and Synthesis

Every Genetic Modification reduces viability — the likelihood of a successful hatch. Hatchery upgrades and the Advanced Equipment Upgrade increase batch size and success rate. At low viability, incubations are more likely to fail and negative traits are more likely to manifest. Use the minimum modifications needed for your current goal and expand as your research progresses.

## Recommended Alpha Trait Targets
1. Aim for Humble + Strong as primary traits (dominance, pack calming, combat edge)
2. Alternatively: Social + Strong (pack-wide comfort boost, dominance, combat)
3. Synthesise a batch; select the egg showing the desired Alpha trait combination
4. Reset genome modifications before synthesising pack members
5. For pack members, target Skittish where possible and avoid all dominance-boosting traits
